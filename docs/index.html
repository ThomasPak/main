Further down you can find a short summary of the Coding dojo and all session reports as blog entries. If you want to know more about what we do at the Coding Dojo check out the About us section. If you want to see which experts and which masterclasses are available check out the Expert board. Check out our GitHub page for the Code we have generated during the problem solving sessions here

The DTC Coding Dojo is a network of peer support established within the Doctoral Training Centre where DPhil students can come with their computer related problems and get help from within our program. Anyone from within the DTC can attend the problem solving session which are held using a laptop and a projector in the Rex Richards Building. The person who submitted the problem will work at the laptop but we will guide you through the process and answer any question that arise on the way. No problem is too miniscule and nothing too ridiculous to submit to the dojo. We all come from very different fields and nobody can know everything within the biosciences.

You can submit your problem until Monday 5pm of the same week using the problem submission form or if you feel you have some computer related expertise we can benefit from let us know through the expert form (Experts will be paid at demonstrator rates if they agree to help during a session)
